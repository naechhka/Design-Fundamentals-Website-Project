Contrast

My first design poster for True/False festival primarily relies on contrast principle. According to my concept, there are several main points of emphasis on this poster which clearly demonstrate contrast. For instance, black film roll creates difference between itself, light yellow background and light grey map of America. Black roll, in turn, contrasts with the text. Content on the roll has difference in size which help to attract attention and emphasises that it is a film festival. Additionally, text uses colour contrast highlight the most important information, further enhancing the overall effect.

Repetition

Second poster uses slightly different colour pallet compared to previous one. The main colours here will be black, red which is the same with red with logo, shadows of the red, white. To demonstrate repetition principle, I repeatedly used the festival name, “True/False”, which quickly captures attention. The colours repetition, in turn, allowed adhere to main concept Additionally, the poster applies another design principle: contrast. This can be seen in the background, which is divided diagonally, with one side red and the other black.

Alignment

This poster is based on alignment principle, where I decided to locate text in the centre to create a sense of order and harmony. Film strip, for its part, is also vertically cantered.

Proxomity

The final poster, which I found the most challenging to implement because the main idea was to place the text in the shape of camera, and it seemed hard to do. This design illustrates the proximity principle by grouping related elements together. I divided content into four groups: title, main paragraph, navigation information and logo. By carefully organizing these elements, I achieved a composition that visually mimics a camera when viewed as a whole. In addition, poster demonstrates elements of contrast principle by using white text colour and black background.
